# Business Requirements – Insurance Claims Transparency & Trust Dashboard

## Problem
Insurance claims often feel like a "black box." Customers don’t know why claims are delayed or denied, and providers lack visibility into performance. This lack of clarity reduces trust in insurers.

---

## Goals
1. Increase transparency in claim outcomes and timelines.  
2. Reduce SLA breaches and unexplained denials.  
3. Provide provider-level performance benchmarking.  
4. Build a foundation for trust between insurers, customers, and providers.  

---

## Scope
**In-Scope:**  
- Claims intake → processing → decision (Approved / Denied / Pending)  
- Metrics: SLA breaches, denial reasons, provider performance, transparency score  

**Out-of-Scope:**  
- Premium billing  
- Policy underwriting  
- Pricing models  

---

## Success Metrics
- Reduce SLA breaches by **15%**  
- Improve Transparency Score by **10 points**  
- Reduce unexplained denials by **20%**  

---

## Key KPIs
- Approval / Denial Rate  
- Average Processing Time  
- SLA Breach %  
- Denials by Reason  
- Provider Transparency Score (0–100)  

---

## Users
- **Operations Manager** → wants to reduce delays and bottlenecks  
- **Provider Representative** → wants to know top denial reasons  
- **Customer Support Analyst** → needs clear explanations for customers  
- **Business Analyst** → needs transparent KPIs for stakeholders  

---

## Assumptions
- SLA target = 14 days  
- Transparency Score is calculated using a simple, explainable formula  

---

## Risks
- Poor data quality → mitigated by cleaning pipeline  
- Misinterpretation of KPIs → mitigated by clear dashboard notes
